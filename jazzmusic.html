JAZZ MUSIC

INTRODUCTION

Violin, Guitar, Drum, and Piano

Jazz music, originating in the early 20th century in African American communities, is known for its rich and complex blend of improvisation, rhythm, and expression. It incorporates a wide variety of influences, including blues, ragtime, and European classical music. The instruments commonly used in jazz include the saxophone, trumpet, piano, double bass, and drums. Each instrument plays a crucial role, contributing to the genre's signature syncopated rhythms and harmonic richness. Jazz continues to evolve, embracing elements from various genres and cultures, making it a timeless and dynamic art form.
